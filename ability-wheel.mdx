The Ability Wheel is a radial pop-up menu that gives you fast access to a larger pool of abilities than the six hotkey slots allow. Holding **G** (rebindable under the **Circle of Imagination** controls category) opens the wheel centered on your screen; moving your cursor over a segment highlights the ability in that slot, and releasing G (or clicking) fires it instantly. The wheel supports up to **16 slots** (`MAX_WHEEL_SIZE = 16`) and is stored separately from your six hotkey bindings — it's a fully independent layer of quick-access on top of your normal ability keys.

## Using the Wheel

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hold G to open the wheel">
    While in-game (not in any other menu), press and **hold** the G key. The screen darkens and the radial wheel appears centered on your display. The game continues running — the wheel screen does not pause.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move your cursor to the desired segment">
    Each configured slot is arranged evenly around the wheel. Hover over a segment to highlight it; the ability's name appears in the center of the wheel. Empty slots show a `+` placeholder and cannot be activated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Release G to activate">
    Let go of G while a slot is highlighted to send the ability activation to the server. You can also click the highlighted slot. If you release G while the cursor is over the center dead-zone (or over no slot), no ability fires and the wheel simply closes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Configuring Wheel Slots

Wheel ability bindings are stored in the same `config/coi_abilities.json` file as hotkey bindings, but in a separate section. The keys `wheel1` through `wheel16` hold each wheel slot's `id - englishName` string:

```json
{
  "ability1": "sun-9-0 - Solar Flare",
  "wheel1": "fool-3-1 - Silver Smile",
  "wheel2": "priest-5-0 - Holy Shield",
  "wheel3": "tyrant-7-2 - Iron Will",
  "wheel4": null
}
```

You assign wheel slots through the same **Ability Binding screen** (K) as hotkey slots. Click the **Show Wheel Mode** toggle button near the top of the screen to switch the dropdown list from hotkey slots to wheel slots. The wheel supports pagination when you have more than 6 slots configured — use the `<` / `>` buttons or scroll to move between pages.

The **active number of wheel slots** displayed during gameplay is controlled by the `wheelSlots` setting in your HUD config (default: **8**, range: 2–16). Even if you have filled all 16 entries in `coi_abilities.json`, only the first `wheelSlots` are shown on the radial. Adjust this in the [HUD Settings screen](/hud-configuration) to match how many slots you actually want on your wheel.

## Wheel vs. Hotkey Slots

Both systems let you activate abilities, but they serve different use cases:

| Feature | Hotkeys (Z–N) | Ability Wheel (G) |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| Number of slots | 6 | 2–16 (configurable) |
| Activation speed | Instant keypress | Hold G → aim → release |
| Best for | High-frequency abilities | Situational or secondary abilities |
| Keybinding requirement | One key per slot | Single key for all wheel slots |

<Tip>
  Use both systems together for maximum flexibility. Bind your most-used combat abilities to the Z–N hotkeys for instant one-key activation, and fill the wheel with situational, utility, or rarely-used powers you still want accessible without hunting through a menu. This way you can carry up to 22 abilities ready at all times — 6 on hotkeys and up to 16 on the wheel.
</Tip>
